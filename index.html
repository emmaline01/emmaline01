My Role:
As a research assistant I storyboarded new features and designed low and high fidelity prototypes for our rebranded mobile app, Cheerpeer. It adds on to the existing app (Peerdea) not only by allowing entrepreneurs to share feedback on creative ideas, but also by guiding entrepreneurs to achieve their goals by themselves through regular self reflection. Project Context:
Peerdea is a mobile app for IOS and Android that allows entrepreneurs to share designs and feedback on their creative work with each other. Cheerpeer builds on this by also supporting entrepreneurs in reaching their individual goals through regular reflection. In collaboration with the feminist makerspace Prototype PGH, over 3 years, 31 entrepreneurs have used Peerdea through an incubator.

Snake Robot Locomotion - Directional Compliance

×

My Role:
I ran experiments comparing robot locomotion with/without various types of directional compliance locomotion by collecting, graphing, and analyzing data on feedback from sensors on the robot. I noticed correlations between torque sensor readings and when the robot hit obstacles, and developed a mass-spring-damper controls system that regulates the snake robot's amplitude based on those torque readings.

Project Context:
This snake robot requires pushing off of obstacles in order to propel itself forward, but complying with obstacles when they get in the way. Directional compliance determines when the robot should be pliable and when it should push against obstacles.

Cardistry Dashboard

×


I created this dashboard as a personal project to support myself through the process of practicing cardistry. I had extremely minimal knowledge of front-end/back-end technologies prior to this, and also wanted to explore making my own web app. It recommends and displays YouTube tutorials of new moves for me to learn using the Google Data API, and when I'm learning moves I can add, edit, or remove them from a Microsoft Azure database. Finally, the dashboard suggests combinations of moves for performing using features of the moves in the database and a Markov chain.

Ukulelebot

×

My Role:
I helped design and CAD (Computer-Aided Design) the 3D-printed and laser-cut parts, then assembled. I also co-led the Arduino programming for the controls. The Arduino can take in a MIDI (Musical Instrument Digital Interface) note and move the linear actuators and servos to the correct positions on the ukulele to play that note.

Project Context:
This robot has two components, of which only one is shown. The image has 4 linear actuators with servos attached for pressing down on the strings of the ukulele, which the second components has one servo to strum. The next steps are finishing assembly for the second part of the robot and coordinating both parts to play the ukulele.
